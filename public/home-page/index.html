About Us

Discover with us Algérienne des Eaux - Ain Defla Unit

Algérienne des Eaux (ADE) is a public industrial and commercial establishment established by Executive Decree No. 01-101 on April 21, 2001. It operates under the supervision of the Ministry of Water Resources and is headquartered in Algiers.

The company has legal personality and financial autonomy and is made up of 15 zones and 44 units, organized to effectively manage water resources throughout the country. These zones were established by Presidential Decree No. 07-173 of June 4, 2007, to enhance coordination and oversight at the regional level. The Ain Defla unit is located in the municipality of Ain Defla, in the Koussider neighborhood, which includes 160 housing units. It serves as general administration and storage for the unit. The establishment implements and applies the national drinking water policy by managing production, transportation, treatment, storage, distribution, and water supply. In Ain Defla province, the Local Environmental Management Unit manages 22 of the 36 municipalities, with a coverage rate of 58. The unit has 70 wells with a daily production capacity of 173,5578 cubic meters per day, 6 treatment plants with 105,062 cubic meters per day, 46 pumping stations with 393,125 cubic meters per day, and 145 reservoirs with a total capacity of 124,230 cubic meters. It employs 924 employees, including 173 managers specializing in various fields such as hydraulics, electromechanics, electronics, chemistry, biology, and information technology.
